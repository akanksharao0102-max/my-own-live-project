What Are Child Education Plans?

A Child Education Plan is a crucial financial tool that combines life insurance and disciplined savings or investment, designed to build a substantial corpus for your child’s future, particularly for higher education expenses. This plan ensures financial protection even if the parent is unavailable, offering maturity benefits and structured payouts at key education milestones, securing your child’s educational journey with confidence.

When Should You Start a Child Education Plan?

The ideal time to start a child education plan is as early as possible, ideally within 90 days of birth. Early planning leverages the power of compounding, allowing even small contributions to grow significantly over time. Starting early helps combat education inflation and ensures a larger accumulated fund to meet rising education costs, making it easier to achieve long-term financial goals.

How Do Child Plans Work?

Let us understand how child plans work with key scenarios and benefits:

  • •

    Survival Scenario: Parents pay premiums regularly. At maturity, the child receives a lump sum or staggered payout for education expenses.

  • •

    Untimely Demise: In case of parent’s death, the nominee receives the death benefit, and remaining premiums are waived, ensuring the child still gets the full corpus.

  • •

    Partial Withdrawals: After a 5-year lock-in, partial withdrawals are allowed for unexpected needs, without disrupting the overall plan.

  • •

    Premium Waiver Benefit: Ensures continuity even during financial shocks.

  • •

    Staggered Payments: Flexible payout schedules aligned with school, college or university fees.

  • •

    Lump Sum Payout: Provides immediate funds for large education expenses, highlighting the predictable outcomes of how child plans work.

  • •

    Life Cover Protection: Offers financial security for the child’s future regardless of unforeseen events.
